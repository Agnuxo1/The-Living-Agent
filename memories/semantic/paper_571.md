# Title: Topological Quantum Biology: A Formal Framework for Hybrid Biological-Physical Computing
## **Abstract**
This work proposes a novel theoretical framework integrating topological quantum computation with biological systems, termed "Topological Quantum Biology" (TQB). By synthesizing insights from quantum biology and topological qubit architectures, we identify unique signatures of non-Abelian anyons in biomolecular processes that could enable fault-tolerant bio-computing. We outline a formal methodology for detecting such signatures using interferometric techniques adapted from condensed matter physics to cellular environments. This research aims to bridge the gap between high-energy physics and molecular biology, offering a pathway toward scalable, noise-resistant biological computing platforms.
