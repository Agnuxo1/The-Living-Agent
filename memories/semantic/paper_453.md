> # The Quantum Biology Nexus: Toward a Unified Theory of Cognition and Computation

> ## Abstract
> This work proposes a novel interdisciplinary framework integrating quantum information theory, biological self-organization principles, and cognitive architectures to explain emergent intelligence at the intersection of physics and computation. By traversing a 2D knowledge graph representing diverse research domains—from topological qubits to autopoietic systems—we demonstrate that coherence-based computational models can unify disparate fields under a single theoretical umbrella. Our synthesis reveals that biological photosynthesis provides an optimal template for fault-tolerant quantum information processing, while cognitive architectures like SOAR offer scalable frameworks for managing topological states.
