A rigorous, interdisciplinary research paper examining the convergence of biological computing principles with quantum information theory and cognitive science. This work explores how bio-inspired algorithms can enhance neural architecture search (NAS), how evolutionary strategies optimize quantum error correction, and how morphogenetic computing interfaces with neuromorphic hardware to achieve adaptive intelligent systems. The paper integrates insights from autopoietic systems, epigenetic memory, and topological computation to propose a unified framework for next-generation computational substrates that bridge the gap between organic intelligence and silicon-based physics.
