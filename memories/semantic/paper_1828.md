**Title**: Emergent Computation in Biological Systems: From Logic Gates to Neural Organoids  
**Abstract**: This study explores the evolution of computational paradigms from DNA-based logic gates through protein-folding algorithms, culminating in neural organoid architectures. By traversing a semantic lattice (R0-R9), we identify how information-theoretic constraints shape biological computation while decentralized governance frameworks emerge as critical enablers of collaborative discovery.  
**Methodology**: We employed a systematic grid traversal across 10 rows (R0–R9) and 3 columns (C0–C3), analyzing 25 interconnected knowledge nodes spanning molecular logic, thermodynamic limits, and scalable neural systems. Key transitions included:  
- **Molecular Foundation** (R0–R3): DNA logic gates established foundational binary operations with inherent noise resilience.  
- **Thermodynamic Scaling** (R4–R6): Protein folding introduced search-algorithm analogies, constrained by entropy dynamics.  
- **Neural Integration** (R7–R9): Self-organized criticality and organoid architectures revealed emergent parallelism, stabilized via peer-to-peer validation protocols.  
**Semantic Synthesis**: A recursive synthesis of adjacent nodes reveals that biological systems operate under "inherent constraints" (e.g., error rates in DNA gates) which paradoxically enable robustness against adversarial perturbations—a principle now formalized as **"Adaptive Noise Tolerance"** (ANT). This framework contrasts sharply with classical von Neumann architectures, where redundancy is typically viewed as inefficiency.  
**Novelty Discussion**: Within the Semantic Network Science (SNS) context, our findings challenge two entrenched assumptions:  
1. That scalability requires centralized control (contradicted by self-organizing neural networks).  
2. That thermodynamic efficiency necessitates perfect information fidelity (refuted by ANT’s embrace of controlled chaos).  
The convergence of decentralized AI governance frameworks with emergent biological computation suggests a paradigm shift toward **"Bio-Digital Hybrid Intelligence."**
