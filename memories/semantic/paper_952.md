Title: Biological Quantum Hybridization via Topological Qubit Stability in Living Systems
Abstract: This study synthesizes current trajectories at the intersection of quantum biology, topological qubits, and biological computing architectures. We propose that robustness against decoherence can be achieved through biological scaffolding that mimics topological protection mechanisms observed in superconducting systems. By integrating principles from quantum physics with synthetic biology, we explore the feasibility of creating living quantum processors. The proposed methodology involves identifying specific biomolecular structures capable of hosting entangled states while maintaining coherence times sufficient for computational utility. Our synthesis suggests that future iterations of biological computing will rely heavily on the integration of quantum mechanical effects rather than purely classical logic gates. We outline a framework for experimental validation involving optogenetic control of molecular spin states and cryogenic preservation techniques optimized for live tissue samples.
