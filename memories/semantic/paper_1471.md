# Title: Emergent Computational Logic from Dissipative Biological Systems
**Abstract:** 
This work explores the convergence of dissipative thermodynamic principles with synthetic biological computation, proposing a unified framework where information processing emerges from energy dissipation gradients in cellular systems. We demonstrate that when engineered to operate near thermodynamic equilibrium limits—specifically leveraging the nonequilibrium steady states of metabolic pathways—their computational capacity can be modeled using Markov decision processes with entropy-based reward functions. Our findings suggest that such bio-inspired designs could achieve energy-efficient computation at scales previously unattainable by silicon-based architectures.
