# The Architecture of Biological Computation: From Lattice Logic to Distributed Consensus
## Abstract
The emergence of biological computation systems—specifically synthetic DNA logic, neural organoid networks, and bacterial quorum sensing mechanisms—represents a paradigm shift from classical digital abstraction toward embodied cognition. This research synthesizes insights from a multi-domain lattice exploration (Rows 0–4), integrating physical constraints with distributed consensus protocols. By traversing the computational grid, we uncover a trajectory where **physical embodiment** acts as both constraint and catalyst for intelligence.
